Detailed Description

Bilinear/linear expressions for vector-valued FE spaces.

Provides composable weak-form building blocks used to assemble systems whose trial and test functions are vector-valued (multi-component) FE functions:

  • FeExpressionGradContr / grad_contr: diagonal sum. Two overloads controlled by the GradOnTest template parameter:
    • GradOnTest=true (default): \( \sum_k \int_K F \cdot \nabla v_k \, u_k \, dK \) – gradient on TEST. Usage: grad_contr(F, grad(v), u)
    • GradOnTest=false (adjoint): \( \sum_k \int_K v_k \, F \cdot \nabla u_k \, dK \) – gradient on TRIAL. Usage: grad_contr(F, v, grad(u))
  • FeExpressionGradContrBlock / grad_contr_block: single off-diagonal \( (k,l) \) block. Same two overloads:
    • GradOnTest=true (default): \( \int_K F \cdot \nabla v_k \, u_l \, dK \). Usage: grad_contr_block<k,l>(F, grad(v), u)
    • GradOnTest=false (adjoint): \( \int_K v_k \, F \cdot \nabla u_l \, dK \). Usage: grad_contr_block<k,l>(F, v, grad(u)) The trial-gradient form assembles \( T_{ij} = \int_K \phi_i \, F \cdot \nabla \phi_j \, dK \) directly – no IBP, no face terms. This is the transpose of the test-gradient stiffness and is needed for the L2 projection of the curl of a vector field, or the adjoint stiffness in a coupled multi-component system.
  • FeExpressionInnerBlock / inner_block: single off-diagonal \( \int c \, v_k \, u_l \, dS \) for one (TestComp,TrialComp) pair. Used for matrix-valued face fluxes in linearised hyperbolic systems.

All block expressions integrate over the (TestComp,TrialComp) pair only; other (I,J) blocks return void() and are skipped by the integrator. Mirrors the existing iteration pattern in WeakFormIntegratorSimplexify so no integrator changes are required to support matrix-valued couplings between components.
